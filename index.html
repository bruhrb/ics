Electronic components are defined as any component in an electronical system. They provide various uses such as converting electricity, resisting current, and much more. They are used in everything involving electricity, such as computers, lights, even things like planes use electronic components. This site will discuss the most common and important electrical components that we use.

Battery

Batteries are simple devices used to store electricity. They release it slowly, and can is used as a supply of power when other power options aren't available. They are very common and are found in everything from phones to remote controllers.

Capacitor

Like the battery, capacitors hold energy. But instead of releasing the power slowly, it almost instantly dumps all of it as soon as it gets a chance. They vary extremely depending on their use, and how much power they need to hold.

Circuit

A circuit is a system of electronic components and wires and/or other connective materials to let electricity flow through the system. It is not a single component, but rather a network of them.

Led

An LED, or light-emitting diode, is a small electrical component that emits light when powered by electricity. The light they emit can have varying amounts of brightness, and use varying amounts of power. They are used as lighting, or to just simply display things.

Resistor

A resistor is a tiny electrical component made out of really really long wires curled up together into a small space. They are used to resist the amount of power in the circuit to prevent circuits from burning out or damaging itself because of power excess.
